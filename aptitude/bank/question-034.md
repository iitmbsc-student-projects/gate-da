For positive real variables $p$ and $q$, if

$$
\log(p^{2}+q^{2})=\log p+\log q+2 \log 3
$$

then, the value of $\cfrac{p^{4}+q^{4}}{p^{2}q^{2}}$ is


- [ ] 81
- [ ] 9
- [ ] 83
- [ ] 79

::: {.callout-note title="Answer" collapse=true}

- [ ] 81
- [ ] 9
- [ ] 83
- [x] 79

:::

::: {.callout-note title="Soluton" collapse=true}

We have:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\log\left( p^{2} +q^{2}\right) & =\log p+\log q+2\log 3\\
 & \\
\log\left(\frac{p^{2} +q^{2}}{pq}\right) & =\log 9
\end{aligned}
$$

Multiplying both sides by $\displaystyle 2$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\log\left(\frac{p^{4} +q^{4} +2p^{2} q^{2}}{p^{2} q^{2}}\right) & =\log 81
\end{aligned}
$$

Since $\displaystyle \log$is a one-one function, we have: 

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{p^{4} +q^{4}}{p^{2} q^{2}} +2 & =81\\
 & \\
\frac{p^{4} +q^{4}}{p^{2} q^{2}} & =79
\end{aligned}
$$


:::
